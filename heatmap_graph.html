Google Stock Data and GDP Growth Rate Correlation

This heatmap shows the correlations between individual company volume metrics and GDP growth rate for each MAANG company between 2006-2013. Apple and Amazon showed the strongest external company correlations with each other in the low 0.9 range, along with Amazon and Google in. Additionally, Amazon and Netflix, and Microsoft and Google, hold high correlations with each other in the low 0.8 range. These paired correlations indicate a shared sensitivity to market-wide trends. However, the GDP growth rate displays only moderate to weak correlations with each company's stock prices, ranging from 0.25 to 0.41. Company share volume has negative correlations with stock price, indicating that changing stock volumes do not necessarily result in price changes. Overall, these correlations suggest that while economic growth supports stock performance, other factors like company innovations and market sentiment may play a more significant role.
